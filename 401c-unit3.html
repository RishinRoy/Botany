Opening note
01

Introduction

Psilotum, Lycopodium, Selaginella, Equisetum, Marsilea and Pteris are important representatives of pteridophytes. They are seedless vascular cryptogams with a dominant sporophytic plant body. However, they show great variation in external morphology, internal anatomy and reproductive structures. Psilotum represents a primitive rootless form, Lycopodium and Selaginella represent lycopods, Equisetum represents horsetails, while Marsilea and Pteris represent ferns. Their comparison shows the evolutionary diversity among pteridophytes.

D · Short comparative summary
05

Short Comparative Summary

Psilotum

Most primitive among these genera because it lacks true roots and true leaves and has a simple protostelic vascular system.

Lycopodium

More advanced than Psilotum because it has true roots, stems, microphyllous leaves and strobili, but it remains homosporous.

Selaginella

Resembles Lycopodium in having microphylls, but it is more advanced because it is heterosporous and has ligules and rhizophores.

Equisetum

Distinct because of its jointed stem, whorled branches, scale leaves and terminal strobili.

Marsilea

An aquatic heterosporous fern with sporocarps, showing adaptation to water and an approach towards seed habit.

Pteris

A typical terrestrial homosporous fern with large fronds, dictyostelic rhizome and sori on the leaf margins.

Final answer ending
06

Conclusion

Thus, Psilotum, Lycopodium, Selaginella, Equisetum, Marsilea and Pteris show wide variation in morphology, anatomy and reproduction. The comparison shows an evolutionary progression from primitive rootless forms like Psilotum to more organized lycopods, horsetails and ferns. Heterospory in Selaginella and Marsilea is especially important because it indicates advancement towards the seed habit, while Pteris represents the typical homosporous fern condition.

Exam-use line: This comparison shows progressive advancement from simple protostelic, rootless forms to heterosporous and dictyostelic fern-like forms among pteridophytes.
